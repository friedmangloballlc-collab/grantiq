## P0 — Critical (resolve before next paying customer)

### P0-1: Unauthorized Practice of Law — nonprofit formation services

**Current exposure**
The `/signup/nonprofit` flow prepares Form 1023/1023-EZ, articles of incorporation, and bylaws. `/terms §6` disclaims legal advice but **the service itself can still constitute UPL in most states**, and disclaimers do not cure UPL — only the safe harbors of state statutes do.

**Case law driving the risk**
- **Unauthorized Practice of Law Comm. v. Parsons Tech.**, 179 F.3d 956 (5th Cir. 1999). Texas won an injunction against self-help legal software until the state legislature enacted a software safe harbor at Tex. Gov't Code §81.101(c).
- **Medlin v. LegalZoom**, Wake Cty. NC Super. Ct. 2015 — NC State Bar pursued LegalZoom for years; LegalZoom had to register as a "prepaid legal services plan."
- **Florida Bar v. Brumbaugh**, 355 So.2d 1186 (Fla. 1978) — foundational rule: non-lawyer may **type** what client dictates; cannot **advise** on form selection or legal consequences.

**The line**
Fill-in-the-blank **as scrivener** is OK. The moment the product recommends which form ("you should use 1023-EZ because your revenue is under $50K") or customizes bylaws ("add this clause because of your state"), it has crossed into UPL.

**States with highest enforcement risk**
1. North Carolina — N.C.G.S. §84-4
2. Texas — Gov't Code §81.101
3. Florida — post-Brumbaugh doctrine
4. California — requires Legal Document Assistant (LDA) registration at the county level under Bus. & Prof. Code §6400, with $25K bond
5. Ohio — Cleveland Bar Ass'n v. CompManagement, 104 Ohio St.3d 168

**Recommendation (pick one)**
- **A. Restructure to "Filing Assistant + Checklist" only** (safest, free):
  - Remove AI-generated bylaws or articles recommendations
  - Eliminate 1023-EZ eligibility recommendations (provide IRS worksheet only)
  - Add Texas-style safe harbor language to every formation screen
  - Rename SKU "Nonprofit Formation Filing Assistant"
  - Geo-block CA and NC until LDA/equivalent registration is obtained
- **B. Partner with Harbor Compliance or similar** (white-label revenue share, zero UPL exposure). Their legal team handles the compliance.
- **C. Build an attorney network** (LegalZoom / Rocket Lawyer model). ~$50K+ setup; not worth it at current scale.

**Priority:** 30 days. **REQUIRES EXTERNAL COUNSEL** for CA LDA determination.

### P0-2: Success fee → "commercial fundraiser" classification risk

**Current exposure**
The 3–5% success fee on grant awards (see `/terms §5`) may trigger registration as a "commercial fundraiser" or "fundraising counsel" under several state charitable solicitation statutes. Enforcement is real: NY AG Charities Bureau is very active.

**Statutes that could apply**
- **NY Exec. Law §172-a(9)** expressly defines "contribution" to include "any … grant … of … money." Plain text reaches grant awards, not just donor solicitation.
- **CA Gov't Code §12599** — commercial fundraiser = anyone who "for compensation … solicits funds … or … receives or controls funds as a result of a solicitation."
- **CA Gov't Code §12599.1** — "fundraising counsel" = anyone who "plans, manages, advises, consults, or prepares material" for solicitation. AI-assisted grant writing arguably fits "prepares material."
- **FL §496.404–.405** — professional solicitor, $50K bond, contract filing.

**Why the "grants aren't donations" defense is only partial**
Most state solicitation statutes define solicitation as a request to donors or the public. Grant applications are arguably institutional applications, not solicitation. But:
- NY's statute expressly includes grants
- CA §12599.1 applies to anyone who prepares materials for solicitation — AI drafting may qualify
- Foundation grants (private foundations, community foundations) are closer to donations than federal grants

**Recommendation**
1. **Contract language already shipped** in `/terms §5` re-reframing: "technology performance fee" not fundraising commission. Strengthen with:
   - Explicit statement: GrantAQ never takes custody of grant funds.
   - Explicit statement: GrantAQ never contacts funders on customer's behalf.
   - Explicit statement: Customer submits every application in their own name.
2. **Operational guardrails** (non-optional):
   - Never route funds through GrantAQ
   - Never sign applications as agent
   - Never email or call funders on customer's behalf
   - Avoid "we raise money" marketing language (that's the statutory trigger phrase)
3. **Obtain a NY Charities Bureau advisory opinion** before taking NY customers. They issue these on request. Budget ~$3K for outside counsel to draft the request.
4. **Consider a flat-fee SKU** for CA/NY to eliminate the percentage-of-proceeds pattern that most strongly signals commercial fundraiser status.

**Priority:** 60 days before accepting NY/CA customers. **REQUIRES EXTERNAL COUNSEL** for NY advisory opinion.

### P0-3: CAN-SPAM compliance — missing physical address

**Current exposure** (pre-fix)
Email templates via `src/emails/shared-layout.tsx` lacked a physical postal address. Under 15 U.S.C. §7704(a)(5) the FTC requires every commercial email to include a valid physical postal address.

**Penalty**
**$53,088 per email** (FTC 2024 adjusted). A single newsletter send of 10,000 recipients with missing address = $530M theoretical exposure.

**Status**
Fixed in this commit. Email layout now includes:
- Physical postal address (via `NEXT_PUBLIC_MAILING_ADDRESS` env var with a placeholder fallback that the user must set to a real address)
- Unsubscribe link
- "Why you're receiving this" context line

**Action required by user**
Set `NEXT_PUBLIC_MAILING_ADDRESS` in Vercel to a real postal address immediately. The LLC's registered-agent address works. A PO Box or CMRA also works.

## P1 — High-risk (30-day horizon)

### P1-1: Multi-state privacy law compliance

GrantAQ's `/privacy` reads as GDPR-flavored and does **not** satisfy several US state privacy law requirements:

| Law | Missing from current policy |
|---|---|
| **CCPA/CPRA (CA)** | "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" link, "Limit Use of Sensitive PI" link, 12-month lookback, financial incentive disclosure |
| **VCDPA (VA)** | **Appeal process** for denied rights requests (statutorily required) |
| **CPA (CO)** | Universal opt-out / Global Privacy Control honoring commitment (mandatory since July 2024) |
| **CTDPA (CT)** | Appeal process |
| **Oregon OCPA** | Specific third-party recipient list on request (stricter than other states) |
| **TDPSA (TX)** | Statutory notice language if sensitive or biometric data is sold |

**Response time requirement** (all states): **45 days** from request, plus 45-day extension option.

**Recommendation**
Rewrite `/privacy` with a **state-specific rights section** that enumerates each law's requirements. Build a DSAR (Data Subject Access Request) intake at `/privacy/requests` with 45-day SLA tracking in Supabase.

**B2B note**: CPRA removed the B2B exemption effective Jan 2023. Board member and staff info you store qualifies as personal info.

**Priority:** 30 days. Counsel recommended but not required if using a template from Termly or Iubenda.

### P1-2: ADA Title III accessibility risk

**Exposure**
Post-*Gil v. Winn-Dixie* (11th Cir., your jurisdiction) and *Robles v. Domino's* (9th Cir., nationwide reach), digital services are covered by Title III. Serial plaintiff firms run automated scans against SaaS sites weekly. Typical surf-by settlement: $3,000–$10,000 each. Multiple firms can hit you in the same month.

**No federal safe harbor exists.** Published accessibility statements + documented remediation are the best practical defense.

**Status**
- Accessibility statement shipped in this commit at `/accessibility`
- WCAG 2.1 AA target documented
- Contact path (accessibility@grantaq.com) established

**Remaining actions**
1. Add `@axe-core/playwright` CI accessibility tests to block regressions
2. Run Lighthouse accessibility score on every marketing page (target 95+)
3. Pay a contractor for a manual top-10-pages audit ($1,500–$3,000) within 60 days
4. Skip overlay widgets (accessiBe, UserWay) — courts rule against defendants relying on them (*Murphy v. Eyebobs*)

**Priority:** 30 days to CI tests; 60 days to manual audit.

### P1-4: AI output liability allocation

**Current**
`/terms §3` and `/terms §4` disclaim AI accuracy and put responsibility on the user.

**Gap**

- Require review timestamp in the export log

### P1-6: Chargeback clause strength

**Current**
`/terms §7` states filing a chargeback without contacting support violates Terms and triggers account termination plus success-fee collection.

**Reality**
Visa/Mastercard/Amex merchant agreements **supersede our Terms**. Consumers have unconditional chargeback rights. Stripe processes chargebacks regardless of our clause.

**What the clause actually does**
- Allows post-facto account termination (useful)
- Provides a basis for collection of outstanding success fees owed (useful)
- Does NOT prevent the chargeback itself

**Strengthening recommendation**
- Add friendly-fraud evidence collection process (log email deliveries, usage timestamps, AI draft completions)
- Configure Stripe Radar rules to auto-submit evidence
- Document evidence collection in the contract

**Priority:** 30 days. No counsel needed.

### P1-7: California auto-renewal law (BPC §17600)

**Requirements**
- Clear and conspicuous disclosure at time of initial offer
- Affirmative consent acknowledgment
- Easy cancellation (cannot require more steps than signup)
- Annual reminder for subscriptions > 1 year

**Penalty**
Up to $2,500 per violation. Active plaintiff class actions in California.

**Current gap**
Unclear if signup flow meets §17602 acknowledgment standard.

**Recommendation**
- Review signup and billing pages against BPC §17602 checklist
- Add explicit "By subscribing you agree to auto-renewal at [price] until canceled" acknowledgment
- Ensure cancellation flow is equivalent to signup

**Priority:** 30 days before first CA customer.

### P1-8: Dispute resolution jurisdiction mismatch

**Current**
`/terms §10` requires AAA Consumer Rules arbitration, Georgia choice of law.

**Gap**
AAA Consumer Rules require filing in the consumer's home state. The Georgia clause may be unenforceable for out-of-state consumers. California courts routinely strike choice-of-law clauses that deprive consumers of local statutory protections.

**Recommendation**
- Align choice of law with AAA Consumer Rules
- Consider JAMS as alternative provider
- Add a "consumer may choose local small claims court" exception

**Priority:** 30 days. **REQUIRES EXTERNAL COUNSEL**.

## P2 — Medium-risk (90-day horizon)

### P2-1: AI training / copyright exposure
Customer AI drafts may use training data including copyrighted grant samples. *NYT v. OpenAI* and similar cases create downstream risk.
**Action:** Document training data provenance. Get representations from LLM providers about clean training data. Include indemnification in any LLM vendor contract.

### P2-2: Limitation of liability ($100-or-12-month cap)
Aggressive but common for SaaS. May be struck down as unconscionable in CA/NY.
**Action:** Raise floor to $10,000 to survive unconscionability challenge. Keep 12-month cap.

### P2-3: Arbitration / mass arbitration defense
Post-*Viking River Cruises* (2022), mass arbitration used to pressure settlement (Uber, DoorDash).
**Action:** Add mass arbitration defense (batched procedures, delayed filing fees). Carve out IP/trade secret disputes from arbitration.

### P2-4: Data breach notification plan
47 states + DC have breach notification laws. Timing 30–90 days. GrantAQ stores EIN, financials, board info.
**Action:** Draft incident response plan. Designate incident response officer. Map each state's requirements.

### P2-5: Partner / referral program
`/partners` page exists. FTC endorsement rules require disclosure of material connection.
**Action:** Add endorsement disclosure clauses to partner agreement. Require partners to disclose "paid GrantAQ partner" in shared content.

### P2-6: COPPA — minors under 13
Nonprofits may have youth programs; forms could capture minor data.
**Action:** Add "no minors under 13 may submit data" clause. Age-gate any youth-specific features.

### P2-7: Cross-border / international customers
If any customer is in EU/UK: GDPR. Canada: PIPEDA.
**Action:** Geo-restrict signup to US (IP-based) OR fully implement GDPR. Easier: restrict to US.

### P2-8: Trademark & domain protection
GRANTAQ not yet registered with USPTO. Typosquatting possible (grantiq.com redirect exists but not defensively owned).
**Action:** File USPTO trademark application ($350–$750). Register defensive domain variants.

### P2-9: Insurance
Tech E&O, cyber liability, D&O. No current coverage documented.
**Action:** Quote with Embroker, Vouch, Coalition. Budget $2K–$5K/year for E&O + cyber at early stage.
