### 2. Follow-Up Cadence (Multi-channel, Automated)

**The sequence:**
1. Email Day 0 (high opening, low pressure)
2. Wait, monitor opens
3. Email Day 3 (social proof)
4. Email Day 7 (scarcity)
5. LinkedIn Day 10 (warm channel)

**Automation:** CRM handles email sequencing, calendar, task assignments.
**Human touch:** Sales person personalizes Day 1 email, conducts all calls, writes proposals.

### 3. Discovery Call Script & Qualification Framework

**The framework: A-Z Qualification**

Each prospect is scored on 6 criteria (0-100 scale):
- **A (Authority):** Is this person a decision maker? (16 pts max)
- **B (Budget):** Do they have $250K+ org budget? (17 pts max)
- **C (Capacity):** Can they dedicate 10+ hours/month? (16 pts max)
- **D (Deadline):** Do they have a grant deadline 30-120 days? (16 pts max)
- **E (Entity):** Are they 501c3, social enterprise, or small business? (16 pts max)
- **F (Fed-Ready):** Are they SAM.gov registered or willing to get compliant? (17 pts max)

**Decision logic:**
- Score 90+: IMMEDIATE CLOSE (call/consult this week)
- Score 75-89: PRIORITY CLOSE (consult, address weak area)
- Score 60-74: NURTURE 30 days (email drip, follow-up)
- Score <60: DISQUALIFY or annual outreach

**Result:** Only pursue qualified leads, save time on bad-fit prospects.

---

### 4. CRM Pipeline (7 Stages)

Sales prospects move through this pipeline:

```
PROSPECT (Email opened)
    ↓
QUALIFIED LEAD (Passed A-Z check in discovery call)
    ↓
CONSULTATION SCHEDULED (Consultation call on calendar)
    ↓
CONSULTATION COMPLETED (Had the consultation)
    ↓
PROPOSAL SENT (Sent formal proposal)
    ├→ WON (Signed, payment received) ← END
    ├→ NURTURE (Not ready yet) → Loop back in 30-90 days
    └→ LOST (Disqualified, competitor, etc.) → End
```

Each stage has:
- Clear entry criteria
- Required actions for sales person
- Automatic triggers for follow-ups
- Exit rules (when to move to next stage)

**Automation:** CRM auto-calculates probability (0% for Prospect, 100% for Won), generates reports, alerts manager when high-value deals move stages.

---

### 5. Lead Scoring Model (A-Z Framework)

Every lead gets a score (0-100) based on:
- How much authority they have
- Whether they have budget ($250K+)
- Whether they can implement
- Timeline urgency (30-120 day sweet spot)
- Eligibility (nonprofit, social enterprise, small biz)
- Federal grants readiness

**Why this matters:**
- Sales person doesn't waste time on $100K nonprofits (too small)
- Doesn't call people without authority (waste of time)
- Doesn't pursue leads without deadline urgency (too soft)

**Result:** Higher conversion rate (50%+ on consultations because we only qualify the right people).

---

### 6. Automation Workflows (What's Automated vs. Human)

**Automated (CRM does this):**
- Email sequencing (send Email 1 → wait → send Email 2 → wait → send Email 3)
- Scheduling discovery call reminders
- Tracking email opens, clicks, replies
- Lead scoring calculations
- Task assignment (e.g., "Call this person")
- Calendar integration
- Pipeline reporting

**Human-Touched (Sales person does this):**
- Research each prospect (5 min, LinkedIn + nonprofit research)
- Personalize Email 1 (first email is always human-written)
- All discovery calls (20-30 min, real conversation)
- All consultations (45-60 min, selling + objection handling)
- Proposal writing (tailored to prospect's situation)
- Closing (getting signature, addressing final objections)

**Hybrid:**
- Email templates (CRM has template, sales person personalizes)
- Follow-up emails (template sent auto, but sales person can customize)
- Proposal generation (CRM generates template based on service tier, sales person tailors)

### 7. SaaS-to-Consulting Pipeline (Free Users → Paid Consulting)

GrantAQ free SaaS users are a warm lead source.

**Triggers for consulting outreach:**
- Logged in 5+ times in first 7 days (engaged)
- Exported source list (serious about research)
- Attempted proposal draft (ready to write)
- Viewed pricing page (budget conversation starting)

**Email sequence:**
1. Email 1 (Day 3 of high activity): "Here's the AI writing option"
2. Email 2 (Day 5): "Why DIY grant writing usually fails"
3. Email 3 (Day 7): "Free grant audit offer"

**Result:** 3-5% of active SaaS users convert to consulting leads (warm lead source, higher conversion than cold email).

## Why This Works

### 1. Heavy Personalization
Every cold email is hand-researched (5 minutes per email). Shows recipient we studied their org, not blast email. Results in 3-5x higher reply rates.

### 2. Qualification Framework (A-Z)
We only pursue leads that meet ALL 6 criteria. Eliminates time-wasters. Higher conversion because we're only talking to buyers who can actually buy.

### 3. Multi-Touch Cadence
First email = 20% open rate, 0% reply rate (just awareness). Third email = 30% open rate, 3-5% reply rate. Persistence pays off.

### 4. Warm Lead Source (SaaS Users)
Free SaaS users who are actively using the product are 3-5x more likely to buy consulting than cold email. Build both channels.

### 5. Clear Sales Funnel
Every prospect has a defined path (PROSPECT → QUALIFIED → CONSULTATION → PROPOSAL → WON). CRM automation handles reminders, follow-ups. Sales person focuses on selling, not admin.

### 6. Data-Driven Optimization
A/B test every variable (subject line, CTA, email length). What works gets scaled; what doesn't gets killed. Continuous improvement.

### 7. Skill-Building
First sales reps learn on cold email (hardest channel). By Month 4, they're closing $5-15K deals. Skills compound.
