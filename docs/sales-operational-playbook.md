```

├─ [ ] Found organization name + size (website/LinkedIn)
├─ [ ] Found prospect name + title (LinkedIn)
├─ [ ] Researched 2-3 recent grants or programs (website/news)
├─ [ ] Identified pain point relevant to their sector
└─ [ ] Noted any personal details (tenure, background) for follow-up

PERSONALIZATION
├─ [ ] Added correct first name (no nicknames, use formal name)
├─ [ ] Inserted organization name in correct context (not forced)
├─ [ ] Matched pain point to their sector (not generic)
├─ [ ] Selected relevant case study or example
└─ [ ] Reviewed email 2x for typos before sending

CRM SETUP
├─ [ ] Created contact in CRM
├─ [ ] Tagged with: COLD_EMAIL, [SEQUENCE_TYPE], [SECTOR]
├─ [ ] Set sequence: Email 1 → Day 0
├─ [ ] Scheduled Email 2 → Day 3 (auto, if no reply)
├─ [ ] Scheduled Email 3 → Day 7 (auto, if no reply)
└─ [ ] Set phone call reminder → Day 14 (if still no response)

TRACKING
├─ [ ] Subject line logged (for A/B analysis)
├─ [ ] List source logged (to track channel quality)
├─ [ ] Expected send date noted
└─ [ ] Goal outcomes defined (open rate, reply rate, call booked)

FINAL CHECK
├─ [ ] Email reads naturally (not too salesy)
├─ [ ] One clear CTA (answer question, click link, or reply)
├─ [ ] Under 150 words (short = better response)
├─ [ ] Signature includes: Name, Title, GrantAQ Consulting, Phone, Website
└─ [ ] Manager reviews before send (first 10 emails from new SDR)
```

## Consultation Call Script (45-60 minutes)

**Read this script before your consultation call. Adapt to their specific situation.**

### OPENING (5 minutes)

```
[Warmly, like catching up with a colleague]

"Hi [First Name], thanks for being here. I know your time is valuable.
I'm excited to dig into this with you.

Here's how I'm thinking about today: In the first 15 minutes, I want to
understand your grants situation deeply. Then, we'll map out exactly
which sources fit you best. And finally, we'll talk about what working
together would look like.

Sound good? [Pause for yes] Great.

Before I ask you a bunch of questions, let me tell you what I'm hearing:
[Recap their pain point + motivation from discovery call].

Is that still the core issue? Or has something changed?"

[Listen—this warm-up builds trust and confirms you listened to the discovery call]
```

### SITUATION ANALYSIS (15 minutes)

```
"Help me understand your current state. Walk me through:

What does your current grant process look like?
[Listen. Document: Who handles grants, how much time, current sources]

How many grants do you pursue per year, and what's your success rate?
[Listen. Document: Volume, win rate, average award size]

What does your funding look like beyond grants?
[Listen. Document: Other revenue streams, grant dependency %, budget constraints]

If I could change one thing about your grants right now, what would it be?
[Listen. This is their core pain. Remember this.]"

[Notes: This section confirms their need is real and helps you position the solution]
```

### SOLUTION DESIGN (20 minutes)

```
"Based on what I'm hearing, here's what I think makes sense:

You need [Service Tier Name], which would [Specific outcomes].

Here's what that includes:
[Detail scope, timeline, deliverables, and why it fits their situation]

The investment is [Price], and here's why:
[Justify price based on ROI: grants won, time saved, risk reduced]

Timeline: We'd start [date] and deliver [date].

Now, before I go further: Does this feel like the right direction?
[Pause. Listen.]

If yes → Move to close.
If hesitant → "What concerns you most?" [Address objection]
If no → "What would make this make sense?" [Reposition]"

[This section transitions from discovery to sell—critical moment]
```

### CLOSE & COMMITMENT (5 minutes)

```
If they're in:
"Perfect. Here's next:
1. I'll send a formal proposal by [date]
2. Review it and let me know if questions
3. Once you sign, we'll kick off with [First milestone]

I typically ask for [Payment terms]. Does that work?"
[Get to yes/no]

If they're unsure:
"I hear you. Here's what I suggest:
Let me send over a one-pager with the exact scope and pricing.
Review it, and let's hop on a quick call Friday to make sure it's clear."

If they're out:
"No problem. Here's what I'll do:
I'll stay in touch. If your situation changes, reach out anytime.
In the meantime, [Free resource or offer]."
```

### FOLLOW-UP EMAIL (Same day, within 2 hours)

```

```

## Objection Handling Scripts

### Objection #1: "We don't have budget for this"

```
Sales: "I hear you. Totally fair question. Let me ask a different way:

If I could show you grants that would fund themselves 3x over,
would budget be the constraint, or would you be interested?

[Listen]

Most of our clients see ROI on their investment within the first 30 days
after they win their first grant. For a $250K grant, investing $1.5K
is obvious math.

Can I ask: What would make this feel like a worthwhile investment?"

[This reframes budget from "we can't afford it" to "is this worth it"]
```

### Objection #2: "We need to talk to our board first"

```
Sales: "Good call. I like that you loop in stakeholders.

Quick question though: In your view, do you think this makes sense?
[Listen—if yes, continue; if no, address real objection]

Here's what I suggest:
I'll send you a one-pager that explains the ROI + timeline.
You can share that with your board. Then, let's reconnect [date]
and address any questions they have.

The proposal won't expire. We can move at your pace.

Does that work?"

[This removes urgency, builds trust, acknowledges their process]
```

### Objection #3: "We want to try it ourselves first"

```
Sales: "I totally get that. DIY makes sense if you want to explore.

Real talk though: 90% of organizations we work with tried DIY first,
got rejected, then realized it wasn't effort—it was strategy.

Here's what I suggest instead:
Spend $750 on our Grant Audit. We review your last 2-3 proposals
and tell you exactly why they were rejected.

That usually answers the question: Is this effort or strategy?

If it's strategy, you'll want our help. If it's effort, you can DIY.
But the audit gets you clarity in 2 weeks.

Interested?"

[This offers lower commitment, proves value, removes risk]
```

### Objection #4: "Your competitors offer this cheaper"

```
Sales: "I believe you. Price-only comparisons are easy.

What I'd ask: Are they also [Specific differentiator—we've worked with nonprofits like yours / We have 10+ years of grant wins / Our success rate is 60%+].

Cost is part of the decision. But if Company X is cheaper but has
no track record with [Your sector], that's a risky bet.

Here's what I propose: Let me show you our case study with a
[similar org] that won [amount]. See if the quality + results justify
the investment.

Fair?"

[This shifts conversation from price to value]
```

### Objection #5: "We're still exploring, not ready to commit"

```
Sales: "Totally understand. Grant decisions take time.

Here's what I'll do: No pressure to commit now. But let me suggest a
starting point that's lower-risk.

Our Grant Audit ($750) is perfect for explorers. It tells you:
1. Are your past rejections fixable? (Strategy or effort?)
2. Which sources make sense for you?
3. What's the realistic ROI of full grant strategy?

It's like a diagnostic. You'll know within 2 weeks if this is worth
investing in further.

Want to start there?"

[This gives them a no-risk entry point]
```

## Sales CRM Workflow Checklist

**Use this to ensure nothing falls through the cracks.**

### Daily Workflow

```
MORNING (15 minutes):
├─ [ ] Check CRM for overnight emails (replies to sequences)
├─ [ ] Move any replies to "WARM_LEAD" status
├─ [ ] Schedule calls for prospects who replied
├─ [ ] Alert manager if anyone scoring 90+ points
└─ [ ] Prepare for discovery calls today (review prep sheets)

MIDDAY (20 minutes):
├─ [ ] Send cold emails (50 per week = 10 per business day)
├─ [ ] Personalize each one (5 min per email)
├─ [ ] Log subject line used (for A/B testing)
├─ [ ] Tag each prospect with relevant fields
└─ [ ] Review open rates on yesterday's emails

AFTERNOON (30 minutes):
├─ [ ] Make discovery call (20 min call + 10 min notes)
├─ [ ] Fill out scoring sheet immediately after call
├─ [ ] Send follow-up email same day (within 2 hours)
├─ [ ] Update prospect status in CRM (QUALIFIED / NURTURE / DISQUALIFIED)
└─ [ ] If qualified: Schedule consultation for next week

EVENING (10 minutes):
├─ [ ] Log activities in CRM (calls, emails sent)
├─ [ ] Update deal stage for active proposals
├─ [ ] Check if any proposals need follow-up (sent 3+ days ago)
└─ [ ] Flag anything blocking tomorrow's activities
```

### Weekly Workflow

```
EVERY TUESDAY (Planning):
├─ [ ] Review last week's pipeline (new leads, moved stages, closed deals)
├─ [ ] Identify hot leads (scoring 80+) needing call this week
├─ [ ] Adjust cold email list (add new sources if needed)
└─ [ ] Plan consultations for next week (calendar fully booked?)

EVERY THURSDAY (A/B Testing):
├─ [ ] Analyze cold email performance (50+ emails sent this week)
├─ [ ] Compare subject lines: Which got best open rate?
├─ [ ] Compare sequences: Which got best reply rate?
├─ [ ] Update winning subject line for next week
└─ [ ] Document results in shared "A/B Test Results" sheet

EVERY FRIDAY (Reporting):
├─ [ ] Run pipeline report (by stage, by source)
├─ [ ] Log closed deals + revenue
├─ [ ] Calculate conversion rates (Discovery → Consultation %, Consultation → Close %)
├─ [ ] Forecast next 30/60/90 days based on pipeline
└─ [ ] Share with manager (5-min standup or email)
```

## Pricing & Positioning Quick Reference

Use this when pricing comes up in calls.

```
SERVICE TIER MATRIX:

Tier 1: GRANT SOURCING ($750, 1-2 weeks)
├─ Best for: Organizations exploring grants for first time
├─ Includes: 20-30 curated sources, prioritized by fit + deadline
├─ ROI: Saves 20-30 hours of research
└─ Pitch: "Let us do the research. You focus on applications."

Tier 2: GRANT AUDIT ($750, 1-2 weeks)
├─ Best for: Organizations with 2-3 past rejections
├─ Includes: Analysis of why proposals failed, recommendations
├─ ROI: Reveals if problem is strategy or execution
└─ Pitch: "Figure out what's blocking you before you spend more time."

Tier 3: GRANT STRATEGY ($1,500, 2 hours + async work, 4 weeks)
├─ Best for: Organizations planning 3+ grants in next 12 months
├─ Includes: Grant landscape mapping, prioritization, 12-month roadmap
├─ ROI: Reduces proposal drafting time by 40%, improves success rate
└─ Pitch: "Have a strategy before you start writing."

Tier 4: FEDERAL COMPLIANCE SUPPORT ($1,000-$3,000, 4-6 weeks)
├─ Best for: Organizations pursuing federal grants for first time
├─ Includes: SAM.gov registration support, DUNS verification, compliance audit
├─ ROI: Prevents disqualification due to compliance errors
└─ Pitch: "Federal funding requires compliance. Let's handle it right."

Tier 5: GRANT WRITING PACKAGE ($5,000-$15,000, 8-12 weeks)
├─ Best for: Organizations ready to invest in winning grants
├─ Includes: Sourcing + strategy + 4-6 written proposals
├─ ROI: Each proposal win typically yields $250K-$500K+
└─ Pitch: "Turn your grant opportunities into funded wins."

---

UPSELL LOGIC:

Prospect interested in Tier 1 (Sourcing)?
→ After they receive sources, follow up: "Ready to start writing?"
→ Offer Tier 5 (Writing) at $7K-$10K to write 2-3 of the best sources

Prospect interested in Tier 2 (Audit)?
→ After they receive audit, follow up: "Want help implementing the fixes?"
→ Offer Tier 3 (Strategy) + Tier 5 (Writing) as bundle ($4K-$8K)

Prospect interested in Tier 3 (Strategy)?
→ After they receive roadmap, follow up: "Ready to start execution?"
→ Offer Tier 5 (Writing) to write roadmap's top-priority sources

Standard close structure:
├─ Tier 1/2 → Tier 3 → Tier 5 (over 3-6 months)
├─ Average total value per customer: $3,000-$20,000 (over 12 months)
└─ Best customers do multiple tiers: Sourcing → Writing → Follow-up audit
```

## Red Flag Checklist (When to Disqualify)

```
If ANY of these are true, consider moving to NURTURE or DISQUALIFY:

[ ] Not a decision maker + refuses to introduce decision maker
[ ] Explicitly says "No budget for professional services"
[ ] Vague timeline + says "someday" or "maybe next year"
[ ] Not eligible (individual, for-profit without B-Corp, etc.)
[ ] Has worked with competitor + satisfied (too risky to convert)
[ ] Hostile tone or dismissive of grant strategy value
[ ] 5+ emails sent, 0 opens (email bouncing or wrong address)
[ ] Opened 3+ emails, 0 clicks, and declined meeting when offered

Action if red flag:
├─ Add to nurture sequence (low-touch)
├─ Set 90-day contact check-in
├─ Don't spend more time on cold outreach to them
└─ Keep for annual re-engagement

DON'T DISQUALIFY if:
├─ Right person but wrong timing (nurture for 30 days)
├─ Budget constraint but strong strategic fit (offer lower-tier service)
├─ SAM.gov not registered but willing to get compliant (offer compliance support)
└─ Still thinking (follow up 1-2x more, then nurture)
```

## Quick Scripts Bank

### "Qualifying Out" Script (Polite Exit)

```
"I appreciate the time. It sounds like you're [specific blocker: no timeline / no budget / exploring, not ready].

Here's what I'll do: I'll stay in touch. If [specific trigger: your timeline accelerates / budget opens up / you're ready to move], just reach out.

In the meantime, here's something free that might help: [Free resource].

Best of luck with your grants."
```

### "Assumptive Close" Script

```
"This seems like a fit. Let me send you a calendar link. Pick a time
that works best for a 45-minute consultation. We'll dive deep into
your specific situation and come out with a clear roadmap."

[Don't ask if they want to schedule—assume they do]
```

### "Proof Point" Script (When They Doubt Value)

```
"I totally get the skepticism. Here's what I'd say:

We worked with [Similar Org Type] in [their sector]. They had a similar
hesitation. After one grant win of $[Amount], they realized the investment
in [our service] paid for itself 10x over.

That's what we typically see. One funded grant covers our cost many times over."
```

### "Scarcity" Script (When They're Hesitant)

```
"I want to be transparent: We have two slots open in [month] for new clients.
After that, we're booked through [future month].

If you want to move forward, we'd need to start by [specific date].
Does that timeline work for you?"
```
