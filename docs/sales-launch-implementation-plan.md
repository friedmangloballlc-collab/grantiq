# GrantAQ Sales Launch: 90-Day Implementation Plan

**Timeline:** 0-90 days from start
**Goal:** Build a repeatable sales machine that generates 50+ cold emails/week, 10+ discovery calls/week, and closes $50K+/month in consulting revenue

---

## Phase 1: Foundation (Week 1-2)

### Week 1: Setup & Hiring

**Days 1-2: CRM & Tool Selection**
- [ ] Evaluate HubSpot vs. Pipedrive (both work; HubSpot has better automation)
- [ ] Purchase licenses: 1 Admin, 1-2 Sales users, 1 Manager account
- [ ] Budget: ~$200/month (HubSpot Sales Hub)
- [ ] Install integrations: Email sync, calendar, Slack notifications
- [ ] Time: 8 hours

**Days 3-4: CRM Configuration**
- [ ] Create all custom fields (see CRM guide, Part 1)
- [ ] Set up 7-stage pipeline with probabilities
- [ ] Create email templates (Emails 1-3 for each sequence)
- [ ] Build automation workflows (see CRM guide, Part 2)
- [ ] Create dashboard views (by stage, by source, by person)
- [ ] Time: 16 hours

**Days 5-7: Hiring (in parallel)**
- [ ] Post SDR/Sales rep job description (if hiring)
- [ ] Screen candidates Week 2 (if hiring)
- [ ] Or: Assign existing sales person to this role
- [ ] Time: 4 hours (ongoing)

### Week 2: Team Training & List Building

**Days 8-10: Sales Team Training**
- [ ] Train sales team on discovery call script (2 hours)
- [ ] Role-play discovery calls (mock prospects, 2 hours)
- [ ] Train on objection handling (1 hour)
- [ ] Train on A-Z qualification framework (1 hour)
- [ ] Train on CRM workflows and daily/weekly processes (1 hour)
- [ ] Time: 7 hours

**Days 11-14: Lead List Building**

Acquire qualified prospect list:
- [ ] Nonprofit sector lists (1,000+ prospects)
  - Sources: DonorTrends, Foundation Center, GiveWell (free/paid)
  - Criteria: 501c3, $500K+ budget, development director/grant manager roles
- [ ] Small business sector lists (500+ prospects)
  - Sources: LinkedIn Sales Navigator, Apollo, Hunter
  - Criteria: <500 employees, $250K+ revenue, in eligible sectors
- [ ] SaaS free user list (in-app exports)
  - Criteria: High engagement (3+ logins), logged in last 30 days
- [ ] LinkedIn prospect list (500+ prospects)
  - Criteria: Title match (Development Director, CFO, Grant Manager), relevant sectors
- [ ] Total target: 2,000+ qualified prospects for Phase 1
- [ ] Time: 12 hours

**Data clean-up:**
- [ ] Remove duplicates (dedupe software or manual check)
- [ ] Validate emails (ZeroBounce or similar to catch invalid emails)
- [ ] Add missing company info (company size, sector, budget data)
- [ ] Time: 8 hours

**Total Week 1-2 Time: ~55 hours** (1.5 FTE week, or two people for half-time)

## Phase 2: Pilot & Optimization (Week 3-8)

### Week 3: Launch Sequence A (High Volume, Quick Wins)

**Tuesday (Day 15):**
- [ ] Send Email 1 to first 100 prospects (Sequence A: "Unfunded Grants")
- [ ] Monitor: Open rate target = 20-25%
- [ ] Monitor: Reply rate target = 3-5%
- [ ] Document: Which emails opened fastest (measure engagement)
- [ ] Time: 4 hours

**Wednesday-Friday:**
- [ ] Monitor opens/clicks every 4 hours (for real-time adjustments)
- [ ] If opens <15%, test new subject line on next batch
- [ ] If replies come in, follow discovery call script immediately
- [ ] Begin scheduling discovery calls
- [ ] Time: 6 hours/day (mostly monitoring, not action)

**By Friday:**
- [ ] Target: 3-5 discovery calls scheduled from first 100 emails
- [ ] Measure: 3-5% reply rate on Sequence A Email 1
- [ ] Action: If >15% reply, scale up. If <3%, test new angle.

- [ ] Launch Sequence B (Federal Compliance) to 50 prospects (mid-market)
- [ ] Hold first discovery calls from Week 3 batch
- [ ] Follow discovery call script; aim for 4-5 qualified leads from 5-7 calls
- [ ] Time: 6 hours

**Daily:**
- [ ] Send 30 cold emails/day (Mon-Fri = 150/week)

**By Friday:**

### Week 5-8: Optimization & Refinement

**Ongoing Activities:**
- [ ] Increase to 50 cold emails/week (target from brief)
- [ ] Hold 10 discovery calls/week (target from brief)
- [ ] Conduct 5 consultations/week (target from brief)
- [ ] Launch Sequence C (AI Writing) for SaaS users
- [ ] Test subject line variations (A/B testing)
- [ ] Test CTA variations (calendar link vs. reply-to-confirm)
- [ ] Test email lengths (is shorter better?)

**A/B Testing Schedule:**
```
Week 5: Subject Line Test
├─ Test A: "Your [Org] is leaving $50K on the table"
├─ Test B: "Are you losing grants to weak positioning?"
├─ Measure: Open rate after 2 days
└─ Winner: Scale; loser: pause

Week 6: CTA Test
├─ Test A: "Reply yes/no if interested"
├─ Test B: "Click here to schedule a 15-min call"
├─ Measure: Reply rate vs. calendar click rate
└─ Winner: Use in all future emails

Week 7: Email Body Test
├─ Test A: 100-word version (current)
├─ Test B: 50-word version (ultra-short)
├─ Measure: Open rate, reply rate
└─ Winner: Implement across all sequences

Week 8: Sequence Selection Test
├─ Test A: 3-email sequence (current)
├─ Test B: 4-email sequence (add urgency email)
├─ Measure: % of prospects replying by email 3 vs. email 4
└─ Winner: Refine and standardize
```

**Key Metrics to Track (Weekly):**
- Cold emails sent: Aim for 50/week
- Discovery calls scheduled: Aim for 10/week
- Discovery calls held: Aim for 8-10/week
- Email open rate: Track and optimize
- Email reply rate: Aim for 3-5%
- Discovery → Consultation conversion: Aim for 40%+
- Average lead score: Should be trending up
- Pipeline value: Should be growing

**Expected Results by End of Week 8:**

### Conversion KPIs (Outcome Indicators)

These show if your process is working.

```
Cold Email Reply Rate:
├─ Target: 3-5% (our personalization should beat industry 2-3%)
├─ How to track: CRM email metrics
├─ Red flag: <2% (subject line or positioning issue)

Discovery Call → Consultation Conversion:
├─ Target: 40% (should be high with A-Z qualification)
├─ How to track: Discovery calls / Consultations scheduled
├─ Red flag: <25% (qualification or discovery script issue)

Consultation → Deal Close Conversion:
├─ Target: 50% (should be high; we only get consultations with qualified leads)
├─ How to track: Consultations held / Deals closed
├─ Red flag: <30% (closing, objection handling, or pricing issue)

Overall Cold Email → Close Conversion:
├─ Target: 1% (50 emails sent → 1 deal closed, eventually)
├─ How to track: Cold emails sent / Deals closed
├─ Red flag: <0.5% (funnel leak somewhere)
```

Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR):
├─ Target Month 1 (Week 1-4): $5K-$10K

Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):
├─ Target: <$500 (should be low since we're doing internal sales)

## Contingency Plans: What If...

### "Email open rates are low (<15%)"

**Diagnosis:**
- Email list quality issue (wrong titles, industries, or email bounces)
- Subject line doesn't resonate (too generic, not personal enough)
- Email coming from unfamiliar sender (build sender reputation)

**Action:**
- Swap 50% of list with higher-quality segment
- Test 3 new subject lines (use curiosity + specificity)
- Add short personalization: "I noticed you lead grants at [Org]"
- Check email deliverability (verify not going to spam)
- Wait 1 week, measure again

### "Discovery calls aren't converting to consultations (low 40% target)"

**Diagnosis:**
- Discovery call script isn't qualifying properly
- Leads are interested but not authority figures
- Pricing not fitting their budget reality
- Unclear about next steps after call

**Action:**
- Record discovery call (with permission), review for objections
- Tighten A-Z qualification (are we being too lenient?)
- Introduce pricing earlier in call ("If this made sense, would you have $X budget?")
- Improve close: "Let's schedule a consultation where we map this out"
- Role-play discovery script with manager

### "Consultations aren't converting to closed deals (low 50% target)"

**Diagnosis:**
- Proposal pricing too high vs. perceived value
- Consultation findings not compelling enough
- Prospect needs more convincing (board approval, budget check)
- Proposal sent too generic (not tailored to their situation)

**Action:**
- Get detailed objections after each consultation ("What would make this a yes?")
- Reposition value: Show ROI math ("This one grant could pay for this service 10x over")
- Offer lower-tier entry product to start ("Start with grant audit, then strategy")
- Send proposal same day as consultation (while energy is high)
- Follow up with case study video or testimonial proof

### "Can't find enough qualified prospects"

**Diagnosis:**
- Lead list quality is poor (wrong titles, industries, or email bounces)
- Prospect pool is too narrow (need to expand sectors or organization sizes)
- Email list already exhausted (need new sources)

**Action:**
- Acquire new list from different source (LinkedIn Sales Navigator, Apollo, Hunter)
- Expand to adjacent sectors (if starting with nonprofits, add social enterprises)
- Expand to adjacent company sizes (start with $500K-$1M, move to $1M-$5M)
- Build prospect list manually from grants.gov winners (get contact info)
- Launch LinkedIn automation to generate warm leads (Dripify, JoinInside)

### "Sales person (or multiple) hit a wall"

**Diagnosis:**
- Sales person is burned out (too many rejection)
- Hitting commission ceiling (compensation not motivating next level)
- Lack of coaching/feedback (not improving)
- Mismatch between their strengths and role

**Action:**
- Review metrics: Are they hitting activity goals (emails, calls) or conversion goals?
- If activity: Role-play script, observe calls, identify skill gap
- If conversion: Likely skill/coaching issue. Get manager involved.
- If burnout: Reduce daily call target short-term, celebrate wins, build motivation
- Consider: Is this person sales or customer success? (Some fit better in implementation)
- Review comp plan: Is commission structure motivating? (Consider tiered bonuses)
