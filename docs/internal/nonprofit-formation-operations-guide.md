## PHASE 1: STATE INCORPORATION

**Timeline:** 1-4 weeks | **Cost to us:** $25-$300 (state filing fees)

### Step 1: Verify Client Information
- [ ] Review intake form data for completeness
- [ ] Confirm proposed organization name is available in client's state
  - Go to Secretary of State website for their state
  - Search business entity database
  - Name must be distinguishable from existing entities
  - Must include designator: "Inc.", "Incorporated", "Corporation", or "Corp."
- [ ] Verify at least 3 board members listed, majority unrelated to founder
- [ ] Confirm officers (President and Treasurer must be different people)
- [ ] Verify mission statement maps to correct 501(c) type

### Step 2: Draft Articles of Incorporation
- [ ] Use state-specific template
- [ ] MUST include these exact IRS-required provisions for 501(c)(3):

**Purpose Clause:**
> "This corporation is organized exclusively for charitable, educational, religious, and/or scientific purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended."

**Dissolution Clause:**
> "Upon the dissolution of this corporation, assets shall be distributed for one or more exempt purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Code, or shall be distributed to the federal government, or to a state or local government, for a public purpose."

**Private Inurement Prohibition:**
> "No part of the net earnings of this corporation shall inure to the benefit of, or be distributable to, its members, directors, officers, or other private persons."

**Political Activity Prohibition:**
> "No substantial part of the activities shall be carrying on propaganda or attempting to influence legislation; the corporation shall not participate in any political campaign on behalf of or in opposition to any candidate for public office."

**Lobbying Limitation:**
> "Notwithstanding any other provision, this corporation shall not carry on any activities not permitted by a corporation exempt under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code."

- [ ] Include registered agent info (use commercial service if needed, $50-150/yr)
- [ ] Include incorporator info
- [ ] Include principal office address (physical, not PO Box)
- [ ] Include initial directors if required by state

### Step 3: File Articles with State
- [ ] File online (preferred — fastest) or by mail
- [ ] Pay filing fee ($25-$125 typical, up to $300)
- [ ] Request expedited processing if available ($50-$500 extra)
- [ ] Obtain 2 certified copies of filed articles
- [ ] Record: filing date, confirmation number, state entity number
- [ ] Note annual report due date for client's state

### Step 4: Obtain EIN from IRS
- [ ] Go to irs.gov/ein → "Apply Online Now"
- [ ] Select "View Additional Types, Including Tax-Exempt Organizations"
- [ ] Choose "Other Nonprofit/Tax-Exempt Organizations"
- [ ] Enter EXACT legal name from Articles (must match perfectly)
- [ ] Enter responsible party info (typically Executive Director or Board Chair — needs their SSN)
- [ ] Complete during business hours (7am-10pm ET, M-F)
- [ ] Save/print CP 575 confirmation notice
- [ ] Record EIN: XX-XXXXXXX

**CRITICAL:** Name on EIN MUST EXACTLY match Articles. Any discrepancy = IRS rejection later.
**WARNING:** Do NOT select "Church" or "Religious organization" unless truly a church.

### Step 5: Draft Bylaws
- [ ] Include all 11 articles:
  1. Name and Purpose
  2. Membership (if applicable)
  3. Board of Directors — number, qualifications, terms (2-3 years), election, removal, vacancies
  4. Officers — positions, duties, terms, election, removal
  5. Meetings — frequency (quarterly minimum), notice (7-14 days), quorum (majority), virtual meeting authorization
  6. Committees — standing, ad hoc, authority
  7. Conflicts of Interest — disclosure, voting exclusion, annual acknowledgment
  8. Finances — fiscal year, banking, audit requirements
  9. Indemnification — director/officer protection
  10. Amendments — process (typically 2/3 vote with advance notice)
  11. Dissolution — must match Articles

### Step 6: Draft Conflict of Interest Policy
- [ ] Required by IRS for Form 1023
- [ ] Must include:
  - Definition of "interested person" and "financial interest"
  - Duty to disclose
  - Procedures for determining and addressing conflicts
  - Board voting exclusion for interested parties
  - Record-keeping requirements
  - Annual disclosure statements for all directors/officers

### Step 7: Prepare Organizational Meeting Minutes
- [ ] Draft minutes template covering:
  1. Call to order
  2. Waive notice of meeting
  3. Ratify Articles of Incorporation
  4. Adopt Bylaws
  5. Elect Board of Directors
  6. Elect Officers
  7. Ratify EIN application
  8. Authorize bank account opening
  9. Adopt Conflict of Interest Policy
  10. Adopt Document Retention Policy
  11. Adopt Whistleblower Policy
  12. Authorize 501(c)(3) filing
  13. Set fiscal year
  14. Set regular board meeting schedule
  15. Adjourn

### Step 8: Deliver to Client (Formation Package)
- [ ] Send completed documents:
  - Filed Articles of Incorporation (certified copies)
  - EIN confirmation (CP 575)
  - Bylaws
  - Conflict of Interest Policy
  - Organizational Meeting Minutes template
  - Document Retention Policy
  - Whistleblower Policy
- [ ] Schedule organizational meeting with client's board
- [ ] If Formation-only package, deliver and close

## PHASE 2: IRS 501(c)(3) APPLICATION

**Timeline:** 2-4 weeks prep + 3-12 months IRS processing | **Cost to us:** $275-$600 (IRS filing fee)

*Only for "Formation + 501(c)(3)" and "Full Grant-Ready" packages*

### Step 9: Determine Form Type
- [ ] Complete 1023-EZ Eligibility Worksheet:
  - Projected gross receipts ≤ $50,000/year for each of next 3 years?
  - Total assets ≤ $250,000?
  - NOT a school, hospital, church, foreign org, supporting org, or credit counseling?
  - If ALL yes → use Form 1023-EZ ($275, 2-4 weeks processing)
  - If ANY no → use Form 1023 ($600, 3-6 months processing)

### Step 10: Prepare Financial Statements
- [ ] If already operating: gather actual financial statements
- [ ] If new: create 3-year financial projections including:
  - Revenue by source (donations, grants, program fees, events)
  - Expenses by category (programs, admin, fundraising)
  - Be conservative and realistic
  - Document all assumptions

### Step 11: Write Narrative Description
**This is the most critical component.** Must address:
- [ ] **WHAT:** Specific programs and services (not vague goals)
- [ ] **WHO:** Who benefits? How are they selected? Open to public?
- [ ] **HOW:** Methods, frequency, delivery mechanisms
- [ ] **WHERE:** Geographic scope and locations
- [ ] **WHEN:** Timeline, frequency, duration
- [ ] **BY WHOM:** Staff qualifications, volunteer involvement
- [ ] **WHY EXEMPT:** How does this further charitable/educational purposes?

**Common mistakes to avoid:**
- "We help the community" → "We provide weekly tutoring to 50 low-income K-8 students"
- Omitting revenue sources or failing to explain fees
- Describing commercial activities without charitable nexus
- Failing to address ALL planned activities

### Step 12: Compile & Submit IRS Application
- [ ] Create account at Pay.gov
- [ ] Select Form 1023 or Form 1023-EZ
- [ ] Complete all parts
- [ ] Upload attachments:
  - Articles of Incorporation (signed, state-stamped)
  - Bylaws (adopted, signed, dated)
  - Conflict of Interest Policy (adopted with signatures)
  - EIN confirmation
  - Organizational meeting minutes
  - Officers/directors list with addresses
  - Narrative description
  - Financial data (actual + projections)
  - Compensation information
- [ ] Pay filing fee ($275 or $600)
- [ ] Submit electronically
- [ ] Record: submission date, Pay.gov confirmation number

### Step 13: Monitor & Respond to IRS
- [ ] Watch for IRS information request letters
- [ ] Respond within 28 days (request extension before deadline if needed)
- [ ] Be thorough and specific in responses
- [ ] When Determination Letter received:
  - Confirm effective date of exemption
  - Store letter permanently
  - Notify client immediately

### Step 14: Deliver to Client (501(c)(3) Package)
- [ ] Send Determination Letter
- [ ] If Formation + 501(c)(3) only, deliver and close

## PHASE 3: GOVERNMENT REGISTRATIONS (GRANT-READY)

**Timeline:** 2-6 weeks | **Cost to us:** $0 (all registrations are free)

*Only for "Full Grant-Ready" package*

### Step 15: SAM.gov Registration

**CRITICAL:** Can start immediately after EIN — do NOT wait for 501(c)(3) approval. This saves 3-12 months by running in parallel.

- [ ] Create Login.gov account (use client's admin email)
  - Go to login.gov → Create account
  - Set up multi-factor authentication
- [ ] Access SAM.gov → Sign in with Login.gov → "Get Started" under Entity Registration
- [ ] Get Unique Entity ID (UEI) — takes 2-3 business days
- [ ] Complete Core Data:
  - Legal business name (EXACT match to IRS records)
  - DBA (if applicable)
  - Physical address + mailing address
  - Start date
- [ ] Select Entity Type: "Nonprofit Organization" → appropriate 501(c) subtype
- [ ] Enter NAICS Code: 813211 (most nonprofits)
- [ ] Enter Financial Information:
  - Bank name
  - Routing number
  - Account number
  - (Account must be in exact legal name of organization)
- [ ] Designate Points of Contact:
  - Government Business POC
  - Electronic Business POC
  - Alternate POC
- [ ] Complete Representations & Certifications (~20 questions)
- [ ] Submit and wait for IRS validation (3-10 business days)

**Common SAM.gov Problems:**

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| IRS Validation Failed | Name/EIN must EXACTLY match IRS records. Wait 2 weeks after EIN issuance for IRS database update. |
| Entity Already Exists | Contact Federal Service Desk (866-606-8220) to merge/deactivate duplicates |
| Bank Account Validation Error | Ensure account is in exact legal name. Allow 1-2 weeks for new accounts. |
| CAGE Code Issues | Auto-assigned for domestic nonprofits. Contact DLA (877-352-2255) if problems. |

**WARNING:** Never pay third parties for SAM.gov registration — it's FREE. Scammers charge $200-800.

- [ ] Set annual renewal reminder (60 days before expiration)
- [ ] Record: UEI number, activation date

### Step 16: Grants.gov Registration

**Prerequisites:** Active SAM.gov registration (must show "Active" status)

- [ ] Go to Grants.gov → Register → Choose "Organization Applicant"
- [ ] Enter UEI (system validates against SAM.gov)
- [ ] E-Business POC from SAM.gov receives admin email
- [ ] Assign at least 2 Authorized Organization Representatives (AORs)
- [ ] Set up user roles:
  - E-Biz POC: Primary admin, manages all users/roles (auto-synced from SAM)
  - Authorized Rep (AOR): Can SUBMIT applications (needs E-Biz POC approval)
  - Standard User: Can create/edit but NOT submit
- [ ] Verify system requirements: Adobe Acrobat Reader, Grants.gov Workspace access

**Best practice:** Assign minimum 2 AORs to avoid submission bottlenecks.
**Warning:** Submit applications 48-72 hours before deadlines — system overloads cause failures.

### Step 17: Grant Readiness Assessment
- [ ] Run GrantIQ Readiness Engine on client's profile
- [ ] Generate first 3 grant matches via GrantIQ Match Engine
- [ ] Prepare Grant Readiness Report for client including:
  - Readiness score (A-J criteria)
  - Top 3 matched grants with match scores
  - Recommended next steps
  - Grant application timeline

### Step 18: Deliver to Client (Full Grant-Ready Package)
- [ ] Send all formation documents
- [ ] Send SAM.gov registration confirmation + UEI
- [ ] Send Grants.gov account credentials/access info
- [ ] Send Grant Readiness Report with 3 matched grants
- [ ] Schedule 30-minute kickoff call
- [ ] Begin 30-day advisory support period

## CRITICAL MISTAKES WE MUST AVOID

| Category | Mistake | Impact | Prevention |
|----------|---------|--------|------------|
| Articles | Missing dissolution clause | IRS rejection | Use template with all 4 clauses |
| Articles | Name mismatch with EIN | IRS rejection | Copy-paste exact name |
| Board | All family members | IRS scrutiny/rejection | Ensure majority unrelated |
| Board | Only 1-2 members | Red flag for IRS | Minimum 3 directors |
| IRS App | Vague activity descriptions | Rejection/delays | Be specific: who, what, where, when, how |
| IRS App | Wrong form (1023-EZ when ineligible) | Auto-rejection | Complete eligibility worksheet first |
| IRS App | Filed 27+ months after incorporation | Lose retroactive exemption | File within 27 months |
| IRS App | No response to info requests | Application closed | Respond within 28 days |
| SAM.gov | Let registration expire | Can't apply for grants | 60-day renewal reminders |
| SAM.gov | Name doesn't match IRS records | Validation failure | Wait 2 weeks after EIN for IRS database sync |
| Grants.gov | Only 1 AOR | Submission bottleneck | Always assign 2+ AORs |
| Grants.gov | Submit on deadline day | System failure risk | Submit 48-72 hours early |
