**Prepared:** 2026-03-29
**Scope:** All 10 revenue dimensions with modeled impact

## Executive Summary

GrantIQ has five active revenue streams generating a theoretical ARPU ceiling of roughly $500/month per SaaS customer when AI writing, success fees, and formation services are included. This analysis identifies nine additional revenue vectors that could increase blended ARPU to $800-$1,200/month per account and create a total addressable revenue pool of $2.4M ARR within 18 months at 200 paying organizations — without adding a single new user.

The highest-leverage untapped opportunities in ranked order:
1. Data products and API licensing
2. Enterprise institutional pricing
3. Grant writer marketplace
4. Training and certification

Vetting: portfolio review, 2 client references, writing sample scored against Friedman Global quality rubric, probationary first engagement.

**Demand side:** GrantIQ users who need human help beyond AI — triggered when AI Writing Expert Review tier selected or when user explicitly requests marketplace referral.

### Take Rate Structure
| Engagement Type | Platform Take Rate | Writer Receives |

| Full-service grant writing | 18% | 82% |
| Grant strategy consultation | 20% | 80% |

### Critical Implementation Note
The Expert Written tier of AI Writing ($1,749) should route directly to the marketplace. Writers receive $1,312 (75%), GrantIQ receives $437 (25%). This converts a fixed-cost product SKU into a scalable marketplace transaction.

## 7. Advertising and Sponsored Listings

### What NOT to Do
- No display ads or banner advertising (makes the product look like a directory, not a platform)
- No selling user behavioral data or email lists (would destroy trust immediately)
- No selling funder listings that influence match rankings (poisons the core value proposition)

### What TO Do

**Sponsored Service Provider Placements**
Contextual placements from attorneys, CPAs, fiscal sponsors, formation services — shown based on current user activity. A user with a "board structure" readiness gap sees a nonprofit governance consultant recommendation.

Pricing: $299-$999/month per category per geography.

**Model:** 5 categories x 3 advertisers x $500/month average = $90,000/year at full inventory.

**Funder Enhanced Profile Pages**
Foundations pay for richer information pages — not to influence rankings. Includes: direct grant portal link, program officer video, FAQ, anonymized interest count.

**Model:** 50 funders at $299/month = $179,400/year.

**Newsletter Sponsorships**
Single sponsored placement per weekly digest, clearly marked. At 5,000-50,000 subscribers.

**Model:** $500/issue x 4 issues/month = $24,000/year at launch scale.

## 8. Expansion Revenue and ARPU Growth

### Expansion Trigger Architecture

**Trigger 1: Grant Win Event**
When a user marks a grant as "Won":
1. Show win amount + "[N]x your annual subscription cost" calculation
2. Prompt upgrade or additional AI Writing drafts
3. Surface the success fee consulting pathway
4. Prompt referral: "Who else in your network needs this?"

Every grant win is an expansion event if engineered correctly.

**Trigger 2: 80% Feature Limit Reached**
Serve in-app upgrade nudge with one-click upgrade and side-by-side comparison. Frame as unlocking capability, not hitting a wall.

**Trigger 3: Deadline Concentration Events**
When 3+ pipeline grants have deadlines within 60 days: "You have 4 applications due in 60 days. Purchase a 5-pack of AI Writing drafts and save 19%." Deadline urgency the platform did not have to manufacture.

**Trigger 4: Annual Renewal Upsell (Month 11)**
Present three options, not a binary:
- Renew current plan annually (save 20%)
- Upgrade to next tier annually (save 20% on higher price)
- Add AI Writing bundle to annual plan (save 25% vs. separate)

### ARPU Growth Model

Path from $90 to $280 ARPU is entirely through product-led expansion, not price increases.

## 9. Annual vs. Monthly Pricing Optimization

### Optimal Annual Discount Structure
| Plan | Monthly | Annual (billed once) | Effective Monthly | Discount |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Starter | $39 | $374 | $31.17 | 20% |
| Pro | $149 | $1,430 | $119.17 | 20% |
| Enterprise | $349 | $3,350 | $279.17 | 20% |

### Strategic Adjustments

**Make annual the default display.** When annual is the default, conversion to annual is 2-3x higher, and annual subscribers churn at 40-60% lower rates. Most competitors including Instrumentl default to annual.

**Introduce a quarterly plan at 10% discount.** Serves grant-season users who are not ready for annual commitment. Better to capture the season at a small discount than lose them after month 1 on a monthly plan.

**Semi-annual plan at 13% discount.** Offer to monthly subscribers at month 3+. Captures annual economics without the full 12-month ask.

### Annual Revenue Impact (200 accounts, blended $149/month)
| Scenario | Monthly % | Annual % | Year 1 Cash | Churn Rate | Year 2 Retained |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| All monthly | 100% | 0% | $357,600 | 35%/yr | 130 accounts |
| Target (50% annual) | 50% | 50% | $474,400 | 22%/yr | 156 accounts |
| Aggressive (70% annual) | 30% | 70% | $519,100 | 18%/yr | 164 accounts |

Moving to 50% annual improves Year 1 cash collection by $116,800. The churn reduction compounds every subsequent year.

## 10. Enterprise and Institutional Pricing

The current Enterprise tier ($349/month, 10 seats) is priced for small teams. It is not appropriate for institutional buyers: government agencies, school districts, CDFIs, community action agencies, hospital systems, university research offices. These buyers have 10-50 users, buy through annual procurement, and are essentially immune to the price sensitivity of individual nonprofit buyers.

### Institutional Tier: Government and School Districts — from $1,500/month
Target: County/municipal governments, school districts, workforce development boards, public housing authorities, tribal governments.

Includes: 25 seats ($30/additional), full API access (100,000 calls/month), custom grant database additions, audit trail exports, federal grant calendar, custom branded reporting, dedicated CSM, 99.9% SLA.

### Institutional Tier: CDFIs and Community Development — from $999/month
CDFIs serve grant-eligible borrowers. GrantIQ's alternative funding engine integrates directly with the CDFI mission.

Revenue model: per-member license ($5-$15/member/month). A CDFI with 1,000 borrowers at $8/month = $8,000/month = $96,000/year from one account.

### Institutional Tier: University and Research — from $750/month
Target: University research offices, technology transfer offices.

Needs: federal grant calendars, PI profile matching to grant opportunities, team collaboration, compliance tracking. These accounts renew automatically if there is no active reason to cancel — minimal churn.

## Priority Execution Sequence

**Immediate (30 days):** Fix the 10 critical platform issues from Master Strategy. Deploy background worker. Wire dashboard to real Supabase data. Without a functioning product, none of this monetization strategy matters.

**Month 1-2:** Launch Training products. Grant Readiness Foundations course: 2 weeks to build. Certification program outline already exists in the 14-module Friedman Global consulting system — converting it to a sellable course is a 4-6 week project. Fastest path to $100K in Year 1 revenue.

**Month 6-12:** Scale advertising and sponsored placements once email list passes 5,000 subscribers and directory reaches 5,000+ grants. Advertising is a later-stage line requiring traffic scale.
