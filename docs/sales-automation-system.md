## 1. Cold Email Sequences (3+ Touch)

### Sequence Structure: 3-Touch Foundation + Expansion

All sequences follow this pattern:
- **Touch 1:** Pain-focused problem statement (high open rates, low CTA friction)
- **Touch 2:** Social proof + credibility (case studies, testimonials)
- **Touch 3:** Urgency + scarcity (deadline, limited capacity)
- **Touch 4+:** Alternative angle or new lead scoring trigger

## 2. Follow-Up Cadence & Multi-Channel Playbook

### Default Email Cadence (5-Touch Framework)

**Pattern:** Email → Wait → Email → Wait → Email → Call/LinkedIn → Email → Close

| Touch | Channel | Day | Subject | Purpose | Note |
|-------|---------|-----|---------|---------|------|
| 1 | Email | 0 | Problem identification | Open rates | Async, low pressure |
| 2 | Email | 3 | Social proof + case study | Trust building | Wait for reply first |
| 3 | Email | 7 | Scarcity + soft CTA | Urgency | If no reply to #1-2 |
| 4 | LinkedIn | 10 | Personalized connection request | Soft re-engagement | Different channel |
| 5 | Email | 14 | Alternative angle (new pain point) | Final cold touch | If no prior engagement |
| 6 | Phone | 14-21 | Coordinated call attempt | Direct relationship | Reserved for ICP-qualified leads |

### Escalation Rules (When to Switch Channels)

```
Email replied? → Pause cold sequence, move to discovery
Email opened 3+ times? → Add LinkedIn message Day 10
Email bounced? → Remove from sequence, flag for research
No opens after 3 emails? → Extend to 5-touch or disqualify
Opened but not clicked? → Resend with different subject line
```

### Multi-Channel Integration: Email + LinkedIn + Phone

**LinkedIn Layer (Inserted at Day 10)**
```
If email opened 2+ times but no reply:

LinkedIn connection request (personalized):
"Hi [First Name], I sent an email about federal grant strategy
for [Organization]. Worth a 15-min call if you're exploring
funding this quarter. —[Your Name], GrantIQ"

Then: Direct message if they accept
"Thanks for connecting. Two quick questions:
1. Are you actively sourcing grants in [your sector]?
2. Is Q2/Q3 a priority timeline?

If no to both, no worries—good to stay connected."
```

**Phone Layer (Day 14-21)**
```
Prerequisite: Email opened 3+ times + LinkedIn message sent

Best times to call:
- Tuesday-Thursday, 10am-12pm or 2-4pm (prospect's local time)
- Avoid Mondays (email noise) and Fridays (check-out mentality)

Script (see Section 3):
"Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name] from GrantIQ.
Did you get my email about [specific topic]?
I know you're busy—this will take 2 minutes."

Outcome:
- If interested: "Perfect, let's get 30 minutes on the calendar."
- If not interested: "No problem. Can I follow up in Q3?"
- If gatekeeping: "I'll send a quick email instead. Thanks."
```

### Special Cadence: "Golden Window" (Post-Discovery Call)

After discovery call scheduled, move to **acceleration cadence**:

| Activity | Timing | Purpose |
|----------|--------|---------|
| Confirmation email + agenda | Next day | Reduces no-shows |
| Pre-call research email | 2 days before | Shows preparation |
| Calendar reminder | 1 day before | Reduces no-shows |
| Post-call follow-up | Same day | Converts to consultation |

## 3. Discovery Call Script & Framework

### Pre-Call (Context Gathering)

**Research checklist before dialing:**
- Organization's website (mission, programs, recent grants mentioned)
- LinkedIn profiles of attendees (role, tenure, background)
- 990 filings or annual reports (budget, programs, staff size)
- Previous grant wins (if you can find them via grantwatch or grants.gov)
- Notable challenges (news articles, social media mentioning funding gaps)

### Opening (First 90 seconds)

```
[Friendly, not salesy tone—like catching up with an acquaintance]

"Hi [First Name], thanks for jumping on this.
I know your time is tight, so I'll keep this focused.

I've been looking at [Organization Name]'s work—specifically your
[recent program or initiative]. It's impressive.

Before we go further, let me ask: What made you take this call?
Was it the grant strategy angle, or something else?"

[Listen—let them talk. This reveals what resonates.]
```

**Purpose:** Builds rapport + immediately gets you insight into their motivation.

---

### Discovery Phase (10-15 minutes)

**Goal:** Qualify or disqualify on A-Z criteria. Use discovery to listen, not pitch.

#### Block 1: Current State (4-5 minutes)

```
"Tell me about your current grant situation.

Are you actively sourcing grants right now, or is this
something you're planning to ramp up?"

[Listen for:]
- Active grants in pipeline (signal of seriousness)
- Budget reality (can they pay?)
- Staffing (who handles grants today?)
- Timeline (urgency level?)

Follow-up based on response:
- If active: "How's that process working for you?"
- If planning: "What's your timeline for getting serious?"
- If passive: "Is there a trigger that would change that?"
```

#### Block 2: Pain Point Mapping (3-4 minutes)

```
"What's the biggest challenge you face with grants right now?

Is it:
- Finding the right sources?
- Getting proposals written?
- Managing compliance stuff (SAM.gov, DUNS)?
- Getting them funded once you apply?
- Something else entirely?"

[Listen—don't interrupt. Map their pain to service tiers.]

Follow-up:
"How often does [pain point] impact your funding?"
"What would solving that be worth to you?"
```

#### Block 3: Budget Reality (2-3 minutes)

```
[Tactful qualification on budget. Must happen before offering solution.]

"Let me ask a practical question:
If we found you a grant source that fit perfectly,
would you have budget to invest in getting it right?"

[Listen to hesitation. This is the signal.]

Follow-up if they hesitate:
"I ask because some orgs are in exploration mode,
and some are ready to invest. Totally different conversations.
Where are you?"
```

#### Block 4: Timeline & Commitment (2 minutes)

```
"If we move forward, when would you want to start?"

[Listen for specificity. Vague = low intent.]

- "Immediately" → High intent, urgent
- "Next quarter" → Medium intent, planned
- "Someday" → Low intent, window shopping

Based on response:
"Got it. So if we put together a plan,
could you execute on it in [their stated timeline]?"
```

---

### Qualification Decision (During/Immediately After Call)

**Use A-Z Qualification Framework:**

| Criterion | Qualified | Partially Qualified | Disqualified |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|--------------|
| **A: Authn./Authority** | Direct decision maker or influencer | Part of team but not final say | Gatekeeper with no authority |
| **B: Budget** | $250K+ org budget; $1.5K+ for grants | $100K-$250K budget | <$100K budget; no budget allocated |
| **C: Capacity** | Can dedicate staff to implement | Willing to learn | No capacity; all stretched |
| **D: Deadline** | Specific grant deadline in 30-120 days | Vague timeline | No timeline mentioned |
| **E: Entity** | 501(c)(3), Social Enterprise, or small business | Exploring nonprofit status | Not eligible (individual, for-profit) |
| **F: Fed-Ready** | SAM.gov registered or ready to comply | Aware of compliance needs | Hasn't heard of compliance requirements |

**Decision logic:**
- **All A-F green:** IMMEDIATE CONSULTATION (move to close today)
- **4-5 of 6 qualified:** SCHEDULE CONSULTATION (this week)
- **2-3 of 6 qualified:** NURTURE TRACK (email drip, follow up in 30 days)
- **0-1 of 6 qualified:** DISQUALIFY (polite close, stay in touch)

---

### Close (Last 2-3 minutes)

**If Qualified:**
```
"Based on what you've shared, I think we can help.
Here's what I'm proposing:

[State proposed service tier based on pain point]

Example 1 (sourcing): "A Grant Sourcing engagement
($750, takes 1-2 weeks) would get you a curated list
of [X] sources that fit [their parameters]."

Example 2 (strategy): "A Strategy Workshop ($1,500, 2 hours)
where we map your grant landscape, prioritize sources,
and build a 12-month roadmap."

Which makes sense for your situation?"

[If yes → Move to consultation scheduling]
[If hesitant → "What would make this easier for you?"]
[If objection → See Section 5 objection handling]
```

**If Partially Qualified:**
```
"Here's what I'm hearing: [Summarize their situation]

I think there's potential here, but I want to be honest—
we'd need [X] to move forward.

My suggestion: Let's schedule a 45-minute consultation
where we dive deeper and figure out if we're the right fit.
Sound good?"

[Move to consultation scheduling]
```

**If Disqualified:**
```
"I appreciate the time. It sounds like you're not quite
ready for what we offer, but that doesn't mean never.

Here's what I'll do: I'll send you a free grant strategy
template and stay in touch. If your situation changes
(timeline, budget, staffing), reach out anytime."

[End cordially, keep in CRM for future nurture]
```

## 4. CRM Pipeline Stages & Lead Status Flow

### 7-Stage Pipeline

```
Stage 0: PROSPECT
├─ Definition: Email opened, no qualification yet
├─ Duration: 0-21 days
├─ Action: Continue email sequence, monitor engagement
└─ Exit: Move to Stage 1 (discovery scheduled) or Stage 7 (disqualified)

Stage 1: QUALIFIED LEAD
├─ Definition: Passed A-Z qualification during discovery call
├─ Duration: 1-7 days
├─ Action: Send consultation proposal, address initial objections
└─ Exit: Move to Stage 2 (consultation scheduled) or Stage 5 (nurture)

Stage 2: CONSULTATION SCHEDULED
├─ Definition: Consultation call is on calendar
├─ Duration: 2-7 days before call
├─ Action: Send prep materials, confirm attendance (1 day before)
└─ Exit: Move to Stage 3 (consultation complete) after call happens

Stage 3: CONSULTATION COMPLETED
├─ Definition: Had the consultation call (30-60 min deep dive)
├─ Duration: Same day, then 1-3 days to close
├─ Action: Send proposal, outline next steps, address objections
└─ Exit: Move to Stage 4 (proposal sent) or Stage 5 (nurture) or Stage 7 (disqualified)

Stage 4: PROPOSAL SENT
├─ Definition: Formal proposal emailed (signed SOW, pricing, scope)
├─ Duration: 3-7 days (follow up every 2-3 days)
├─ Action: "Just checking in" emails, phone calls, pressure testing
└─ Exit: Move to Stage 6 (won) or Stage 5 (nurture) or Stage 7 (lost)

Stage 5: NURTURE
├─ Definition: Not yet ready; waiting for timeline/budget/trigger
├─ Duration: 30-90 days
├─ Action: Drip email sequence, check-ins every 2 weeks
└─ Exit: Move to Stage 1 (qualified again) or Stage 7 (disqualified) after 90 days

Stage 6: WON (Consultation Scheduled → Conversion)
├─ Definition: Signed engagement letter, payment received
├─ Duration: Ongoing (execution phase begins)
├─ Action: Onboarding, project kickoff, delivery
└─ Exit: Completion, upsell opportunity, referral request

Stage 7: DISQUALIFIED / CLOSED LOST
├─ Definition: Budget, authority, timeline, or need not aligned
├─ Duration: Permanent (unless specific trigger returns them)
├─ Action: Add to "stay in touch" list, annual check-in
└─ Criteria: No response after 5 touches, explicit "no", budget constraints, etc.
```

## 5. Lead Scoring Model (A-Z Qualification Framework)

### Scoring System: 0-100 Points

**Calculation:** Sum of A-F criteria (each max 16-17 points), then apply engagement multiplier

#### A: Authority (16 points)
```
16 points: Confirmed decision maker for budget/engagement decisions
12 points: Key influencer, part of decision committee
8 points:  Influencer but not final decision maker
4 points:  Gatekeeper or assistant
0 points:  No authority or unknown
```

**How to verify:**
- Title check: Executive Director, CFO, Development Director = likely authority
- Email body signals: "I'll need to discuss with my board" = influencer, not maker
- LinkedIn seniority: How long in role? Career progression?
- Discovery call signal: "I'm the person who handles this"

---

#### B: Budget (17 points)
```
17 points: Organization >$500K annual budget + allocated grant services budget
14 points: Organization $250K-$500K + some grant funding mentioned
11 points: Organization $100K-$250K, grants are secondary focus
7 points:  Organization <$100K, no budget allocated
0 points:  No budget or unwilling to invest
```

**How to verify:**
- 990 filings (public, for 501c3s): Total revenue on line 12
- Website annual report or impact statement: Shows budget scale
- Discovery call signal: "We budget $X for grants" vs. "We have no budget"
- Explicit question: "If this made sense, would you have $1.5K to invest?"

---

#### C: Capacity (16 points)
```
16 points: Dedicated grant staff (1+ FTE) or clear implementation bandwidth
12 points: Part-time grant person or willingness to dedicate resources
8 points:  Interested but stretched; would need to reorganize
4 points:  Very stretched; would struggle to implement
0 points:  Explicitly stated they have no capacity
```

**How to verify:**
- LinkedIn staff size: How many people work there?
- Org structure research: Is there a grant role?
- Discovery call signal: "We have Sarah who handles grants" vs. "It's on everyone's plate"
- Follow-up: "Can you dedicate 10-15 hours/month to this?"

---

#### D: Deadline (16 points)
```
16 points: Specific grant deadline 30-120 days out (urgency sweet spot)
12 points: General timeline ("this quarter") or deadline 121-180 days
8 points:  Vague timeline ("eventually") or deadline >180 days
4 points:  No specific deadline but exploring
0 points:  No timeline or "we'll get to it eventually"
```

**How to verify:**
- Discovery call: "When do you need this done by?"
- Grants.gov research: Do their sector have seasonal cycles?
- Org research: What year do their programs typically start?
- Signal: Specific dates (9/30, 12/31) = higher urgency than "next quarter"

---

#### E: Entity Status (16 points)
```
16 points: Registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit
14 points: Social Enterprise or for-profit certified B-Corp
12 points: Small business (<500 employees, <$20M revenue)
8 points:  In formation but legitimate (applying for 501c3)
0 points:  Individual, for-profit without B-Corp, or ineligible
```

**How to verify:**
- IRS 501(c)(3) lookup: Type in org name at irs.gov
- SAM.gov lookup: Federal contractor database
- LinkedIn about section: "Nonprofit" or "Social Enterprise" stated
- Discovery call: "Are you a registered 501(c)(3)?"

---

#### F: Fed-Ready (17 points)
```
17 points: SAM.gov registered + DUNS number + compliance-aware
13 points: SAM.gov registered OR DUNS number + aware of compliance
9 points:  Not registered but willing to complete compliance (2-4 week timeline)
5 points:  Compliance-unaware but interested
0 points:  Resistant to compliance requirements
```

**How to verify:**
- SAM.gov lookup: Search their organization name
- DUNS lookup: Via Dun & Bradstreet database
- Discovery call: "Are you SAM.gov registered?" (Immediate signal)

## 6. Automation Workflows: What's Automated vs. Human Touch

### Automation Philosophy
- **Automate:** Repetitive, low-judgment tasks (email sending, scheduling reminders, data entry)
- **Human Touch:** Relationship-building, objection handling, complex problem-solving, consultations
- **Hybrid:** Initial personalization (research), follow-up sequencing (triggered by behavior)

---

### Automation Playbook

#### Automation Layer 1: Inbound Workflow

```
TRIGGER: New email signup in GrantIQ (SaaS)
ACTION (Automated):
  1. Add contact to CRM, tag "SAAS_USER"
  2. Send welcome email immediately
  3. Set tag: "Days since signup" = 0
  4. Wait 3 days

DECISION POINT (Automated Rules):
  If user logged in 3+ times in first 3 days:
    → Add to "HIGH_ENGAGEMENT" segment
    → Send in-app message about consulting services
  Else:
    → Send "How's it going?" email (Day 3)

ESCALATION (Human):
  If user books a calendar call for consultation:
    → Immediately alert sales team
    → Sales team does pre-call research (30 min)
    → No auto-response; sales person owns the call
```

---

#### Automation Layer 2: Cold Email Sequencing

```
TRIGGER: New cold prospect added to "Source A" list
ACTION (Automated):
  1. Day 0: Send Email 1 (problem identification)
  2. Day 3: If no open → Resend Email 1 with different subject
  3. Day 5: If opened Email 1 → Send Email 2 (case study)
  4. Day 7: If no reply → Send Email 2 (resend if not opened)
  5. Day 10: If opened but not replied → Send LinkedIn connection request
  6. Day 12: If no clicks on Email 2 → Send Email 3 (scarcity)
  7. Day 14: If still no reply → Log in system "Ready for phone call"

HUMAN INTERVENTION TRIGGERS:
  - Email replied to: STOP sequence, human sales person takes over
  - Email bounced: Human removes from sequence, researches correct email
  - Email opened 4+ times: Human adds LinkedIn message + phone call
  - Lead opens Email 3 but doesn't click: Human adjusts approach via phone
  - Any "out of office" response: Human manually pauses sequence, resumes after dates
```

---

#### Automation Layer 3: Follow-Up Scheduling

```
TRIGGER: Discovery call scheduled (both parties accepted calendar invite)
ACTION (Automated):
  1. Add to "CONSULTATION_SCHEDULED" stage in CRM
  2. Send confirmation email within 15 minutes (includes call agenda)
  3. Set reminder: 2 days before → "Pre-call research email" (human drafts, auto-sends)
  4. Set reminder: 1 day before → "Confirm attendance" email (automated)
  5. Set task: 30 min before call → Alert sales team
  6. Set post-call task: "Follow-up email required within 2 hours"

HUMAN INTERVENTION:
  - Sales person reviews prospect profile 24 hours before call (30 min research)
  - Sales person handles the actual call (no recording requirement unless requested)
  - Sales person sends post-call follow-up email (custom, not template)
  - Sales person schedules next action based on qualification
```

---

#### Automation Layer 4: Lead Scoring & Segment Assignment

```
TRIGGER: Data updated in CRM (new discovery call note, 990 filing reviewed, etc.)
ACTION (Automated):
  1. Recalculate A-F scores based on latest data
  2. Update lead score in CRM
  3. Reassign segment if score threshold crossed:
     - >85 points: Move to "PRIORITY_CLOSE" queue
     - 60-85 points: Move to "STANDARD_NURTURE" queue
     - <60 points: Move to "LONG_NURTURE" queue
  4. Alert sales manager if lead moves to PRIORITY_CLOSE
  5. Trigger relevant email sequence based on new segment

HUMAN VALIDATION:
  - Sales person reviews score, can override if different signal (e.g., "seemed disinterested on call")
  - Sales manager reviews PRIORITY_CLOSE assignments weekly
  - Quarterly review: Are scoring thresholds working? (conversion data)
```

#### Automation Layer 5: Email Templates & Personalization

```
TEMPLATE SYSTEM (Hybrid):

Structure:
  1. CORE template (same for all, created by marketing)
  2. PERSONALIZATION variables (unique per prospect)
  3. PREVIEW logic (auto-generated merge fields)

Example:
  Template: "Email 2: Case Study"

  Variables:
    [Organization Name] ← Pulled from CRM automatically
    [Org Type] ← Nonprofit / Social Enterprise / Small Business (auto-assigned)
    [Similar Org Description] ← Best matching case study (human selects, template inserts)

  Before sending, sales person:
    - Previews final email (checks personalization)
    - Can edit [Similar Org Description] if template doesn't match
    - Adds subject line A/B variant (auto-tracked)
    - Approves send (no send without human approval)

Automation after send:
  - Auto-track open (yes/no, timestamp)
  - Auto-track link clicks (which links, how many)
  - Auto-track reply (yes/no, move to manual handling)
  - Auto-assign next action based on engagement
```

#### Automation Layer 6: Proposal Generation & Tracking

```
TRIGGER: Consultation completed, sales person marks "Ready for proposal"
ACTION (Automated):
  1. Pull data from discovery call notes (pain point, timeline, budget)
  2. Generate proposal template with:

  3. Create PDF with prospect's organization logo
  4. Save in CRM for human review
  5. Alert sales person: "Proposal template generated, ready for review"

HUMAN INTERVENTION:
  - Sales person edits proposal (customizations, special pricing, unique scope)
  - Sales person writes custom cover email
  - Sales person sends proposal with personal note
  - Sales person tracks opens/clicks on proposal
  - Sales person follows up 2-3 days after send with "Any questions?"
```

```
  2. Every 30 days: Send one nurture email (not pushy, educational value)
  3. Every 60 days: Flag for manual check-in (sales person considers calling)
  4. After 90 days in NURTURE: Alert manager for strategy review

HUMAN INTERVENTION:
  - Sales person can customize drip sequence per lead (e.g., skip emails)
  - Sales person manually checks in if triggered ("Hey, just checking in...")
  - Sales person reassesses score if situation changes
  - Manager reviews nurture effectiveness quarterly
```

## Key Principles (Read These)

1. **Lead with value, not features.** Don't mention GrantIQ features until they ask. Start with their pain.

2. **Personalize using research.** Spend 5 min per prospect researching org size, recent grants, sector trends. It shows and increases response rates 3-5x.

3. **Keep emails short and scannable.** One clear idea per email. No more than 100-150 words. One CTA per email.

4. **Follow-up is where deals close.** First email gets opens. Second email gets replies. Third email gets meetings. Don't give up after one email.

5. **Score leads rigorously.** Only call/consult with A-F qualified leads. Disqualify bad-fit prospects early (saves time, reduces objections).

6. **Automate workflows, not relationships.** Sequence emails, schedule follow-ups, but keep consultations and objection handling human.

7. **Test and iterate.** A/B test subject lines, CTA styles, email sequences. Change one variable per test. Track everything.

8. **Close in the discovery call when possible.** Most deals close because you earned authority and trust in the call itself, not after.
